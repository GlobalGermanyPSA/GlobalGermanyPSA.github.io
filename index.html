Germany: A Country Rich in History and Culture.

Historical Locations

Germany's historical landmarks are representative of the country's great architechture and reputable civil engineering.

Brandenburg Gate, Berlin

Berlin's Most Famous Landmark - Representative of National Unity and Peace.

Constructed by Prussian king Frederick William II, Brandenburg Gate once served as the main enterance into the City of Berlin.

Quadriga, a statue of the godess of victory, was removed from the top of the gate by Napolean when his Grand Army assumed occupation of Berlin.

Neuschwanstein Castle, Neuschwansteinstraße, Schwangau, Germany

A Royal Retreat

Neuschwanstein Castle was a palace of retreat built by Ludwig II of Bavaria who was also known as the "Fairytale King".

The castle was built in inspiration from Richard Wagner, a world-renowned music composer.

You may be tempted to draw comparisons to certain Disney castles. After all, Cinderalla's castle was modeled after it.
